  "summary": "Decentralized social network architectures are defined by four fundamental components that shift control from centralized servers to individual users. Identity is managed through self-sovereign systems, typically utilizing public-key cryptography to ensure users maintain ownership of their digital presence without reliance on a central authority [1]. Storage is handled via distributed or peer-to-peer networks, such as IPFS or decentralized databases, which ensure data availability and resilience against censorship [1], [2]. Messaging protocols facilitate communication between nodes, often employing relay mechanisms or gossip protocols to propagate information across the network in a trustless manner [1]. Finally, consensus mechanisms—ranging from blockchain-based proof-of-stake to federated voting models—are employed to maintain a consistent state of the network, validate interactions, and prevent malicious activity without a central administrator [1], [2].",
